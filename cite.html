Correct usage when citing a title: According to the Wikipedia article University of California, San Diego, UCSD was established in 1960. An example of using <cite> in a bibliography (but remember, only use around the title): Fitzgerald, F S. The Great Gatsby. New York: Scribner Paperback Fiction, 1995. Print. Here is an example if you want to quote an article with its source (remember never to cite the autor as the work title): According to the NCBI article Impact of computer use on children's eyes by N. Kozeis, Extensive viewing of the computer screen can lead to eye discomfort, fatigue, blurred vision and headaches, dry eyes and other symptoms of eyestrain.
